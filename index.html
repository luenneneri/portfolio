overview

While working on the redesign of one of our consumer-facing products, to avoid watching the design team become a bottleneck in the process, I sat down with our front-end developers to align and properly prioritize our next moves. After discussions about the concept and similarities between tokens and atomic design, we were able to define naming and other basic rules to create new components, making the redesign process more efficient and cohesive.

Even though we had tried to develop a system before as a design team, kickstarting with the making of our products' libraries, Lina ‚Äì our design system named after a phenomenal female architect, only became tangible when it meant solving a problem that involved user, business, and execution. By adjusting inconsistencies in design foundations, getting two recently merged teams more familiar with the other product, and fixing duplicate code, we were finally equipped to honor one of the company's values properly: do more agile experiments to gather evidence and insights to build better products.

overview

hourglass is a tool that seeks to facilitate the work/life balance dialogue in the workplace, reducing work-related anxiety through improved communication, setting more explicit expectations for the employees, and shaping a culture that recognizes employees who care for themselves.

The project started with researching different topics for mental and affordable health and how people explore their home cities. In the interview phase for these topics, however, work was the subject that stood out as the leading influencer in decisions related to them. So much so that the word "fatal" was used multiple times to describe career-related failures.

With this problem in mind, the focus was to help companies and managers change their employees' relationships with work. Nonconforming of crooked motivations, such as simply improving employee productivity, this product's vision was to improve communication, provide a sense of community, and promote the non-romanticization of work overload.

After rounds of quantitative and qualitative research, grounded on the evidence and insights drawn from this process, the decision for the MVP was to:

1. facilitate the transparency about the most stressful factors.
2. understand employers' intentions with this tool to make the provision of data more personalized.
3. provide mechanisms for the employees to argument raises and promotions.
4. emphasize the importance of ceremonies such as 1: 1's and dates such as holidays and days off.
5. provide the means to build a proper career plan

overview

Gather was the result of research about women's rights to urban spaces. By continually keeping our eyes on the screen late at night, we're often making ourselves more vulnerable (no, we don't believe that it's our fault).

This app was created to connect women who might share the same paths on their daily routines so that they can get out of their phones and team up to feel safer while commuting.

The product focuses on safer commutes in two ways:

1. Making compatible connections
2. Opt to having someone watching your trip for you

Gather was not conceived as a definitive solution to women's safety, though, as it's seen as a palliative alternative to a very systemic issue. The lack of trust in such products due to bad experiences with apps that addressed gender issues was the other problem.

As noticed throughout the competitive analysis and in the interview phase, many are built prematurely without understanding the actual problem they want to solve, often based on what we know about these oppressions historically added to a deficiency of quality data.

Based on that, Gather data ‚Äì a live document fed by ongoing research ‚Äì would live inside the Gather app, providing an open dataset for those attempting to build better-informed products regarding women's safety in urban spaces.
